The DRY principle stands for "Don't Repeat Yourself."

It's a software development principle that encourages you to avoid duplicating code, logic, or knowledge in multiple places. Instead, you should have a single source of truth for each piece of functionality.

Why DRY Matters
When the same code appears in multiple places:
Bugs are harder to fix because you must update every copy.
Maintenance becomes more expensive.
The codebase becomes harder to understand.
Changes can introduce inconsistencies.

DRY also applies to:
Database schemas: Avoid storing the same information redundantly.
Configuration files: Centralize shared settings.
Documentation: Maintain information in one authoritative location.
Business rules: Implement rules once and reuse them.
